FAQs

How often should I be seeing a dentist?

Our team (along with the American Dental Association) recommends that patients of all ages should plan to attend at least two dental checkups and cleanings each year. These preventive appointments give our dentists the chance to catch and treat problems during their earliest stages, plus they go a long way in preventing the development of many issues in the first place!

When should I schedule my child’s first dental visit?

We encourage parents to schedule their child’s first dental visit as early as six months of age and no later than their first birthday. This visit will consist of the dentist checking their gums and mouth, and it will help set the tone that the dental office is a safe and supportive environment your child doesn’t have to fear. We actually have a certified pediatric dentist on-staff just for taking care of little smiles, Dr. Alex Okoye!

Can you help with a dental emergency?

Yes! If you or a loved one are experiencing a lot of dental pain due to a broken/knocked out tooth or severe toothache, you can always call our office to get the immediate care you need. We’re often able to make same-day appointments for emergency patients, so don’t hesitate to come see us when you need dental care right NOW.

Do I have to get metal braces to straighten my teeth?

Not anymore! For adult and teen patients with misaligned teeth, we’re now able to offer them Invisalign, an orthodontic treatment that completely does away with the traditional bracket and wire system. Instead, it uses a series of clear plastic aligner trays to shift the teeth while completely preserving the natural appearance of the smile. Better yet, the trays are 100% removable as well for easy eating and tooth brushing. To see if this treatment would be a good fit for you, contact us today.
